Event Requests

What Do Members Want Next?

Themes extracted from open-ended comments across both surveys

01

Hackathons & Data Challenges

Most requested by far — 7+ independent mentions across both surveys. Members want to compete, build, and apply skills in a structured challenge format.

02

Workshops & Crash Courses

ML training, deep learning, LLM basics, SQL, report writing. Members are hungry for hands-on skill-building with practical takeaways they can use immediately.

03

Industry & Career Events

Career panels, Student-Faculty Mixer (brought back from Fall 2024), industry speaker workshops. Members want direct connections to the real-world DS job market.

04

Freshmen Onboarding Track

Multiple newcomers asked for "a roadmap on how to start" — which sites to use, how to begin a project, beginner-friendly resources. A dedicated intro track is clearly needed.

05

In-Meeting Mini Activities

Small DS tasks or mini-games during GBMs — icebreakers, small coding challenges, group activities. Makes meetings more interactive and less lecture-heavy.

06

Cross-Campus Collaboration

Collaborations with other UTA departments, external orgs, and alumni. Build a broader Maverick data network that extends beyond club meetings.

recommendations.md
Strategic Insights

Key Takeaways for Leadership

Data-backed recommendations for Spring 2026 planning

01

Proven learning ROI — use it

100% skill confidence uplift is a powerful stat for grant applications, sponsorships, and university recognition. Document this every semester.

02

Run a hackathon — it's the #1 ask

7+ unprompted requests for competitions. A single datathon event could be the highest-impact thing NSDC does in Spring 2026.

03

Formalize officer recruitment

85% leadership interest (35 Yes + 18 Maybe) means the pipeline is full. Create a short application process to convert "maybes" into active contributors.

04

Double down on instructor partnerships

Faculty referrals are #2 at both events consistently. A targeted outreach to 3–4 key DS/CS professors each semester could grow attendance significantly.

05

Build a freshmen onboarding track

Multiple new members explicitly asked for roadmaps. A "Getting Started in Data Science" workshop at semester start addresses the biggest knowledge gap.

06

Plan for member graduation turnover

38% of EOS respondents graduate by Dec 2026. Start mentorship and knowledge-transfer programs now to maintain continuity heading into 2027.
